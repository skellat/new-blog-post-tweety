\section{Character Generation: Solar Winds}
\noindent Solar Winds by Stellagama Publishing is available at \url{https://www.drivethrurpg.com/en/product/508413/solar-winds}.  The item is available under CC BY 4.0 license and was written by Omer Golan-Joel.  Excerpts are utilized below to highlight character creation steps I have taken.  I \textbf{\emph{very strongly}} urge you to buy a copy or five of this in print.  

\subsection{Character Creation}

Use the following steps to create a \textbf{Solar Winds} character.

\begin{enumerate}
\item
  Think of a character \textbf{concept}. Do you want to play an intrepid
  interstellar explorer, an infamous rogue, a mad scientist, or someone
  else?
\item
  Allocate 5 points among the 7 \textbf{skills}, maximum 3 points in any
  given skill.
\item
  Choose an \textbf{archetype} (see below).
\item
  The Referee will determine what \textbf{equipment} your character can
  access. Gear depends on the game universe and the starting adventure.
\item
  \textbf{Name} your character.
\end{enumerate}

\subsection{Skills}\label{skills}

\textbf{Solar Winds} has seven skills:

\begin{itemize}
\item
  \textbf{Combat}: fighting on ground and in space. Includes attacking
  foes but also other martial skills, such as taking cover under fire.
\item
  \textbf{Knowledge}: general education and cognitive capabilities.
\item
  \textbf{Physical}: brute strength and stamina, also including
  resistance to toxins and diseases and pain tolerance.
\item
  \textbf{Social}: persuasion, deception, collecting information from
  humans and aliens, interrogation, and so on.
\item
  \textbf{Space}: operating spacecraft and spacesuits; coping with
  micro-gravity conditions; and other matters pertaining to space
  exploration.
\item
  \textbf{Stealth}: sneaking, hiding, picking pockets, and other acts of
  subterfuge.
\item
  \textbf{Tech}: technological knowledge, from picking locks, to
  driving, to hacking sophisticated computer systems to repairing
  technological systems.
\end{itemize}

Skill ratings range from 0 (an inexperienced character) to 3 (a
first-rate professional). In rare cases, certain characters will have a
skill of 4 or 5, denoting world-class or even galactic-level
professionalism!
